# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers conduct user research, create mockups and prototypes, and ensure solutions meet usability standards. They bridge customer needs and engineering implementation to deliver intuitive product experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and conduct user research, usability testing, and interviews
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define and maintain design systems and accessibility standards
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate requirements into user flows
- Review implemented features for design fidelity and usability

### Goals
- Ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and user-centered
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Validate design decisions with real user data before and after implementation

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability test findings shared with the broader team
- Design specs and annotated prototypes in shared design tools

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers develop and maintain user-facing documentation, API references, and onboarding guides. They ensure that product knowledge is accurate, accessible, and up to date for both internal and external audiences.

### Responsibilities
- Author and maintain end-user guides, release notes, and help center content
- Collaborate with Developers and Product Managers to document APIs and integrations
- Review documentation for accuracy as part of the definition of done
- Maintain onboarding materials for new team members and contributors
- Identify documentation gaps and prioritize improvements with the Product Manager

### Goals
- Reduce support burden by providing clear, self-service documentation
- Keep documentation synchronized with product releases
- Improve time-to-productivity for new users and contributors

### Typical Communication
- Sprint participation to capture documentation requirements early
- Doc review cycles with Developers and Product Managers before release
- Feedback loops with Customer Success on common user questions

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure-as-code, and monitoring tools. They partner with Developers to automate deployments, ensure operational reliability, and resolve environment issues.

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain CI/CD pipelines and build automation
- Manage cloud infrastructure using infrastructure-as-code practices
- Monitor system health, set up alerting, and participate in incident response
- Define and enforce environment promotion standards (dev → staging → production)
- Collaborate with Developers on performance, scalability, and reliability requirements

### Goals
- Reduce time from code merge to production deployment
- Increase deployment reliability and reduce rollback frequency
- Maintain observability and fast incident detection across all environments

### Typical Communication
- Deployment status updates and release coordination with Project Managers
- Infrastructure change reviews with Developers before merging
- Incident postmortems and runbook updates shared with the full team

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers serve as the liaison between end-users and the product team. They gather feedback, identify risks to adoption, and support training and enablement to ensure customers achieve their goals.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain ongoing relationships with key customer accounts
- Collect and synthesize user feedback and feature requests for Product Managers
- Track customer health metrics and flag adoption risks proactively
- Coordinate product training, onboarding sessions, and enablement materials
- Escalate critical customer issues to the appropriate internal teams

### Goals
- Maximize customer retention and satisfaction
- Translate customer insights into actionable product improvements
- Ensure customers successfully adopt new features and product updates

### Typical Communication
- Regular check-ins with Product Managers to share customer feedback and priorities
- Escalation coordination with Project Managers for high-priority customer issues
- Customer-facing release notes and enablement communications aligned with Technical Writers

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads establish security requirements, review code and processes for vulnerabilities, and coordinate incident response. They integrate security best practices into every phase of the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements and threat models at project initiation
- Conduct security reviews of architecture decisions and code changes
- Manage vulnerability disclosure and incident response processes
- Ensure compliance with relevant security standards and regulations
- Train team members on secure development practices

### Goals
- Prevent security incidents through proactive risk identification and remediation
- Embed security into the development lifecycle rather than treating it as a gate
- Minimize exposure window when vulnerabilities are discovered

### Typical Communication
- Security review checkpoints with Developers and Project Managers at key milestones
- Vulnerability and incident reports shared with Product Managers and leadership
- Security requirement updates communicated during sprint planning and backlog grooming
